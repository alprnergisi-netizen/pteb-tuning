# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Performance car owners and enthusiasts in Melbourne and across Australia. They know what they want — specific platforms (Audi EA888, BMW B58, VW Golf R), specific goals (more boost, E85, anti-lag) — and they're hunting for a tuner they can trust. They arrive via Google, Instagram, or word of mouth. Mobile is the primary access point. Their emotional context: excitement mixed with caution — tuning is expensive and permanent, so credibility matters more than flash.

## Product Purpose

PTEB Tuning is Melbourne's specialist ECU/TCU remapping and mechanical workshop for European performance cars. Services span custom dyno tuning, DSG/transmission service (DQ200, DQ250, DQ381), mechatronic repair, engine rebuilding, and performance parts. The site exists to convert visitors into enquiries and bookings.

Core marketing claim: **PTEB has never tuned a car where the engine blew up.** This is a verifiable, specific credibility statement that separates PTEB from tuners who prioritise power numbers over safety margins. Use it prominently and without hedging.

## Brand Personality

Craft · Obsession · Mastery

Voice: a specialist who doesn't need to shout. Confident without being corporate. Technical without being cold. The kind of shop where the founder answers the phone and knows your engine code.

Emotional goal: the visitor should feel they've found someone who actually knows what they're doing — not a generic shop, not a flash brand, but a genuine specialist with receipts.

## Anti-references

Generic auto shop sites: stock photos of wrenches, fake neon racing stripes, cheap clip-art logos, "WE DO IT ALL" copy, generic "performance upgrade" language that could apply to any car. The site should feel like a discovery — not a directory listing.

Also avoid: loud tuner culture sites (flames, chrome text, overloaded effects). PTEB's credibility comes from data and craft, not spectacle.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through specificity.** Generic claims ("we do great work") are worth nothing. Specific data (47 verified reviews, +74 kW on the F82, EA888/DAZA/B58 platform expertise) earns trust. Every claim should be a specific fact or result, not a marketing adjective.

2. **Show, don't tell.** Dyno results, real car photos, real engine codes, real customer names. The work speaks; the copy explains context, not conclusion.

3. **Artisan confidence.** A boutique specialist doesn't explain why they're the best — they demonstrate it. No defensive copy. No comparison tables. Just evidence.

4. **Every word earns its place.** No section headings that restate the eyebrow label. No intros that repeat the title. No filler. The visitor's time is as limited as their patience.

5. **Performance, not flash.** The car metaphor applies to the site: fast to load, precise in information architecture, no wasted motion. Animation that serves comprehension, not spectacle.
